# Director’s Treatment — “I’m Just a Build”

## Logline

A fragile build idea arrives on Silicon Hill as an uncertain parchment character and becomes a legitimate release only after passing through constitutional regulation, explicit constraints, verification, and reproducible proof.

## Concept

The piece translates software governance into a clear, teachable 60-second animated narrative. The protagonist is not a personified lawmaker but a personified build contract: parchment body, inked face, wax seal, slight wobble, expressive but restrained. The world around the character is a simplified server-rack hillscape called **Silicon Hill**. The story is about becoming trustworthy through process, not becoming exciting through spectacle.

## Visual Style

- 1970s educational TV homage
- hand-cut flat-shape feeling
- matte palette
- thick outlines
- stepped, intentional motion
- mild deterministic wobble
- square composition optimized for social teaser format

The image should feel warm, tactile, and legible. It must not feel like modern startup branding, glossy SaaS promo, or AI-generated futurism.

## Palette

- **Cream parchment:** primary character body
- **Ink black:** outlines and facial details
- **Mustard:** dominant environmental accent
- **Olive:** secondary structure and hills
- **Burnt orange:** emphasis and transitional warmth
- **Wax red:** seal only, sparingly used
- **Muted off-white:** captions and paper highlights

## Protagonist Design

The Build is a parchment sheet with:

- slightly rounded corners
- visible title lines suggesting a build contract
- red wax seal near lower-right quadrant
- simple face rendered in ink
- minimal limb language if needed, but body should remain mostly document-like

The Build should read immediately as “paper with authority under construction.”

## Silicon Hill

Silicon Hill is a stacked server-rack landscape:

- rolling hill silhouettes formed from rack blocks
- blinking detail minimized or removed
- no complex perspective
- modular shape repetition to imply compute infrastructure
- skyline acts as governance terrain rather than cyberpunk scenery

## Motion Grammar

- slow pans
- stepped position shifts
- seeded wobble for analog-feeling life
- restrained bounce
- no overshoot-heavy modern easing
- no camera whip, zoom blur, parallax spectacle, or hyper-kinetic transitions

Every movement should feel like a guided classroom diagram coming alive.

## Camera Rules

- mostly locked or gently drifting camera
- front-facing or three-quarter staging
- compositions centered for square readability
- act transitions handled through wipes, slide-ins, or compositional swaps rather than flashy FX

## Act Direction

### Act I — Concept

The Build appears uncertain on Silicon Hill. The environment suggests raw possibility but not legitimacy. The line delivery and visuals should imply: “I’m only an idea unless I’m governed.”

### Act II — Mechanism

Constraint boxes, arrows, constitutional frames, and process channels appear. The Build moves through a machine-legible path. This act should feel instructional: structure arriving, ambiguity shrinking.

### Act III — 10 Pillars

The screen becomes a rhythmic grid or procession of ten pillar cards. Each pillar must read as a necessary part of build legitimacy. The tone is declarative, memorable, and ordered.

### Act IV — Validation / Resolution

The Build emerges stable, stamped, and legitimate. The final image should communicate release through proof rather than triumph through hype. The ending is resolution, not celebration excess.

## Typography

Typography should feel educational and era-adjacent:

- bold, plain, readable
- high contrast
- no futuristic display faces
- no ornate serif overload
- strong all-caps or title-case headline use where clarity benefits

## Tone

Accessible, wry, disciplined, and teachable. The video should respect the seriousness of governance while remaining friendly enough to remember after one watch.
