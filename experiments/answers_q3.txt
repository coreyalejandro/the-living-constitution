QUESTION 3: If a train travels 60 mph for 2 hours, how far does it go?
============================================================
BID ANSWER:
# Backward Instructional Design (BID) Approach

## Step 1 – Identify the Audience & Prior Knowledge
**Who:** A student learning basic distance/speed/time relationships (likely middle school level)
**What they know:** 
- Multiplication basics
- What "mph" means (miles per hour)
- Concept of time in hours

## Step 2 – Define the Final Takeaway
**They must understand:** 
*Distance = Speed × Time* — and be able to apply it to get **120 miles** as the answer.

## Step 3 – Build Backwards from the Takeaway

**Working backwards:**

**Level 4 (The Answer):** 60 mph × 2 hours = **120 miles**

**Level 3 (Why this formula works):** 
- "mph" means the train covers 60 miles in *each* hour
- With 2 hours, you get 60 miles twice: 60 + 60 = 120

**Level 2 (What each number represents):**
- 60 mph = the *rate* (how fast it's going)
- 2 hours = the *time* (how long it travels)
- Distance = what we're solving for

**Level 1 (Foundation concept):**
- Speed tells you how much distance is covered per unit of time
- More time at the same speed = more total distance

---

**The answer: The train travels 120 miles.**

============================================================
COT ANSWER:
# Chain of Thought Solution

**Step 1: Identify what we know**
- Speed of the train = 60 mph (miles per hour)
- Time traveled = 2 hours

**Step 2: Recall the distance formula**
- Distance = Speed × Time

**Step 3: Apply the formula**
- Distance = 60 mph × 2 hours
- Distance = 120 miles

**Final Answer:**
The train travels **120 miles**.
