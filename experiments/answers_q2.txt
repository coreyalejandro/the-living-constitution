QUESTION 2: What caused the French Revolution?
============================================================
BID ANSWER:
# What Caused the French Revolution? (Using Backward Instructional Design)

## Step 1: Identify the Audience & Prior Knowledge
**Who:** High school history students
**What they know:** Basic facts about monarchy, taxes, and social hierarchy

---

## Step 2: Define the Final Takeaway
**Students must understand:** The French Revolution resulted from a **perfect storm** of interconnected crises—financial collapse, Enlightenment ideas, social inequality, and weak leadership—that made the existing system unsustainable.

---

## Step 3: Build Backwards from the Takeaway

### **Level 3 (The Takeaway)**
A system breaks when: demands for change + inability to adapt + loss of faith in leadership = revolution

### **Level 2 (Supporting Pillars)**
Four major causes worked together:

1. **Financial Crisis** → Government bankruptcy from wars and overspending
2. **Enlightenment Ideas** → People questioned why absolute monarchy was necessary
3. **Social Inequality** → Common people paid taxes; nobles and clergy didn't
4. **Weak Leadership** → King Louis XVI couldn't fix problems or make hard decisions

### **Level 1 (Basic Building Blocks)**
- **What is absolute monarchy?** One person (the king) has all power
- **What is the Third Estate?** Commoners who had no political voice
- **What are taxes?** Money people must give to the government
- **What is debt?** When you owe more money than you have
- **What are Enlightenment ideas?** New thoughts that humans have rights and should help govern themselves

---

## The Connection
Without the financial crisis alone → no revolution. Without Enlightenment ideas alone → people wouldn't imagine alternatives. Without inequality alone → people might accept their role. But **all four together** made the old system impossible to maintain.

COT ANSWER:
# Chain of Thought Analysis: Causes of the French Revolution

Let me break down the major factors that led to the French Revolution (1789):

## Step 1: Economic Crisis
- France faced severe debt and financial problems
- Expensive wars (especially American Revolution support) drained the treasury
- Famine and poor harvests increased food prices and poverty
- The tax system was unfair—common people paid most taxes while nobility and clergy were exempt

## Step 2: Social Inequality (The Estate System)
- Society was divided into three estates: clergy, nobility, and commoners
- The Third Estate (commoners) had little power despite being the majority
- Rigid class structure prevented social mobility
- Growing middle class (bourgeoisie) resented their lack of political influence

## Step 3: Political Mismanagement
- King Louis XVI was weak and indecisive
- Government was inefficient and corrupt
- Enlightenment ideas about democracy and individual rights challenged absolute monarchy
- People questioned the divine right of kings

## Step 4: Food Shortages and Starvation
- Bread prices skyrocketed
- Mass hunger created desperation among the poor
- This sparked riots and unrest

## Step 5: Inspiration from Other Revolutions
- American Revolution (1776) showed that revolution could succeed
- Enlightenment philosophers (Voltaire, Rousseau) inspired revolutionary thinking

## Final Answer:
The French Revolution was caused by a combination of **economic crisis, unfair taxation, rigid social inequality, weak leadership, food shortages, and Enlightenment ideals**. These factors together created widespread discontent that exploded into revolution in 1789.
