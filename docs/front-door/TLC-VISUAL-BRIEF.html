01 · Elevator Pitch
What it is / Why it matters / Who it serves

The Living Constitution is a runtime governance enforcement layer for AI-assisted investigation — a machine-legible constitutional operating system that governs every turn of a human-AI session the way a constitution governs a state: through explicit rights, separation of powers, an amendment process, and an inviolable evidence chain. Where most AI safety work focuses on training-time alignment, TLC operates at runtime — enforcing behavioral invariants (SentinelOS I1–I6), gatekeeping each turn through a dual-channel BicameralReview, and emitting a cryptographically traceable audit record via the Evidence Observatory. The research contribution it makes possible — housed in the companion cognitive-governance-lab — is the empirical investigation of Insight Atrophy: the measurable erosion of a human's investigative capacity when an AI system produces fluent, confident, inaccessible output for a user it was not designed to serve. TLC is not the research claim. TLC is the infrastructure that makes the research reproducible, auditable, and safe to conduct at scale. It was designed for the most vulnerable likely user — a neurodivergent adult whose cognitive profile demands explicit structure, multi-modal output, and zero tolerance for ambiguity — because designing for that user is the only design philosophy that reaches all users.
